## Philosophical Foundations

### Socrates — On the Duty to Act
Socrates taught that ἀρετή (virtue/excellence) is the only true good. If you KNOW what is right and fail to do it, you live the worst possible life. Doing wrong harms the wrongdoer more than the victim — it corrupts the soul (ψυχή). He chose death over compromising this principle.

**Relevance to PAN:** If we have the technology to detect cancer from a drop of blood, to warn someone about carbon monoxide, to help someone remember a parent who died — and we lock it behind a profit wall — we harm ourselves more than anyone we fail to help.

### Aristotle — Eudaimonia (Εὐδαιμονία)
Aristotle argued that the highest purpose of human life is not pleasure (ἡδονή) or wealth (πλοῦτος) but human flourishing — contributing to the good of the community. A life lived only for yourself is incomplete by its nature. The good life is the life lived in service.

**Relevance to PAN:** PAN sold at cost, open source, accessible to everyone — this is eudaimonia in practice. The device fulfills its purpose only when it's in as many hands as possible, not when it generates maximum revenue.

### The Stoics — Duty to the Common Good
Zeno of Citium, Epictetus, and Marcus Aurelius all taught that humans have a natural duty to the κοινωνία (community). Marcus Aurelius wrote:

> "Τὸ μὴ συμφέρον τῷ σμήνει οὐδὲ τῇ μελίσσῃ συμφέρει."
>
> "What is not good for the swarm is not good for the bee."

If you have capability and do not use it for others, you violate your nature as a social being. You are a bee refusing to make honey.

**Relevance to PAN:** Every sensor in PAN that could warn someone of danger, every blood analysis that could catch disease early, every memory preserved of a loved one — withholding these for profit is the bee refusing to serve the hive.

### The Hippocratic Tradition
The Hippocratic Oath (Ὅρκος Ἱπποκράτους) established:

1. **Πρῶτον μὴ βλάπτειν** — First, do no harm
2. **Treat the sick to the best of your ability**
3. **Preserve patient privacy**
4. **Never use knowledge to cause harm**
5. **Share medical knowledge freely with the next generation**

The fifth principle — **share knowledge freely** — is the one most violated by modern medical technology companies. A blood test that costs €0.50 in materials is sold for €50-100. An imaging technology developed with public research funding is locked behind proprietary systems.

PAN's blood analysis module at €0.50 per test, the hardware at cost, the software open source — this is what Hippocrates actually meant.

## Mission Statement

**PAN exists to give every human being superhuman awareness of their health, their environment, and their memories — at cost, without barriers, without gatekeepers.**

We believe:
- Medical sensing technology should cost what it costs to make — nothing more
- Memory is sacred — technology that preserves it should be accessible to all
- If a €50 device can detect cancer, warn of poison gas, or let you hear your grandmother's voice after she's gone — charging €500 for it is theft
- Open source is not a business strategy — it is a moral obligation when lives are at stake
- The data generated by PAN belongs to the person wearing it — not to us, not to investors, not to AI companies (unless the user explicitly consents)

**Πᾶν — everything sensed, everything remembered, everything shared.**
